# Meta

- **Description:** Technology company formerly known as Facebook; operates social media platforms and explored fintech initiatives
- **Industry:** Technology, Social Media, Digital Finance
- **Involvement:** Proposed Libra stablecoin (2019, renamed to Diem in 2020, abandoned by end of 2020); subject of regulatory scrutiny

## Key Events
- **June 2019:** Announced [[Libra]] stablecoin proposal backed by consortium of 40 companies
- **June 2020:** Rebranded Libra to Diem; restructured to address Congressional concerns about basket-of-currencies backing
- **End of 2020:** Libra/Diem abandoned due to Congressional and regulatory opposition

## Regulatory Context
- Pre-existing negative reputation due to data privacy and anti-competitive concerns
- Congressional opposition led by [[Maxine Waters]] (House) and [[Sherrod Brown]] (Senate)
- Described by legislators as attempt to create "shadow banking system" and "Swiss-based financial system too big to fail"
- Absence of a viable fintech player at the time meant regulatory attention was concentrated on Meta itself

## Impact on Stablecoin Policy
- Libra's failure paradoxically enabled more favorable stablecoin policy by removing threat-perception and visceral opposition
- Once Meta abandoned stablecoins, the regulatory conversation could shift from "ban this" to "how to regulate this"
