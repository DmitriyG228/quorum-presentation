# Brian Steele (DTCC) & Joe Lubin (ConsenSys) in Conversation

**Date:** 2024-09-20
**Duration:** ~1 hour
**Recording:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BaCbGynWP7g
**Event:** Phase 1: FSI Foundation

## TL;DR
In a fireside chat moderated by [[Michael Casey]], [[Brian Steele]] (DTCC) and [[Joe Lubin]] (ConsenSys) discuss a decade of blockchain adoption in traditional finance. Despite early tensions between centralized trust (TradFi) and decentralized trust (crypto), regulatory clarity, technological innovation (Layer 2 rollups), and DTCC's tokenization initiative are converging to enable a new financial market structure. The conversation covers DTCC's path to tokenizing $100T in assets, SWIFT ledger development, enterprise blockchain evolution, and the integration of AI agents with decentralized protocols.

## Discussion

### The Trust Foundation and Early Days
Both organizations arrived at blockchain through different paths but shared commitment to improving trust infrastructure. Joe discusses the loss of trust in centralized institutions (post-2008 crisis context) and how Bitcoin/Ethereum provided cryptographic solutions. Brian notes DTCC's longstanding role as the ledger for the industry—custodying $100+ trillion in US equities—and the challenge of balancing that responsibility with innovation.

**Key points:**
- [[Rob Palatnik]] was DTCC's early champion for blockchain involvement (starting ~January 2016)
- Joe's perspective: "We've been on the same team all along—we're all just trying to build better systems" (including regulators and law enforcement)
- Both Hyperledger Foundation and Enterprise Ethereum Alliance were early collaborative efforts around shared trust

### DTCC's Digital Assets Journey: From Disruption Anxiety to Enablement
Rather than seeing blockchain as a threat, DTCC frames tokenization as "enabling our clients' digital assets journey" while maintaining existing institutional trust standards. Brian's approach:
- Enable tokenization of the $100T in assets DTCC currently holds
- Integration with multiple Layer 1 networks (not locked into one chain)
- Tokens live in wallets of clients' choosing
- Activity enabled across all integrated Layer 1s
- Bridge TradFi and DeFi without displacing existing actors (300+ direct participants can leverage the service)

**Decision:** DTCC pursuing path to "disrupt ourselves" (per Rob Palatnik's philosophy) while balancing pre-existing legal and regulatory obligations.

### Regulatory Clarity as a Catalyst
**Action:** SEC issued no action letter (just before holiday break in 2024)
- Provided regulatory certainty for DTCC's tokenization work
- Triggered significant client inbound interest
- Confirmed DTCC's approach of creating "compliant-aware tokens"

**Key points:**
- Maintains existing KYC/AML perimeter (not bearer instruments)
- Tokens can transfer between wallets only if registered through the service
- Regulatory clarity enables rational, compliant innovation

### SWIFT Ledger Breakthrough
Joe notes ConsenSys' involvement in SWIFT's response to stablecoin and banking concerns:
- **Context:** SEC administration change (Gensler to Atkins); stable coins growing; banks worried about deposit flight
- **Action:** SWIFT recognized the concern and asked ConsenSys to brainstorm and prototype a "SWIFT ledger"
- **Milestone:** [[Javier Taso Perez]] announced breakthrough project at Cyos Frankfurt; big banks gave permission to move forward
- **Outcome:** "It's been moving really fast from there"

### Enterprise Blockchain Evolution: Dead to Reborn
Joe discusses the journey from Hyperledger/Enterprise Ethereum Alliance experiments to current architecture:
- Early private/permissioned blockchain projects "essentially failed" (insufficient trust models)
- Joe and others "declared enterprise blockchain dead" but "long live enterprise blockchain"
- **New approach:** Rollup-centric architecture on Ethereum Layer 2
- Properly structured Layer 2 rollups inherit full trust guarantees of base Ethereum layer AND enable many different use cases
- This provides "sufficient decentralization" while serving enterprise needs

### Future Market Structure: Beyond Asset Class, Geography, Regulator
Brian describes three traditional dimensions of financial services market structure: asset class (equities, bonds, derivatives), geography, and regulator. In the tokenized future:
- **Regulator** remains
- **Asset class and geography** become democratized—barriers dissolve
- **Example:** Today, equity traders use one venue, futures traders another, swap traders a third—all with separate financing pipelines. In tokenized markets, all can flow through unified structure
- **Outcome:** Capital currently "bottled up" in separate pipelines becomes accessible; massive efficiency gains
- **Process:** Organic, dynamically evolving market structures emerge (market for market structures)

### Tokenization Deep Dive: NFTs as Metadata Containers
Joe envisions NFTs as flexible metadata containers:
- One NFT = one Apple share, or 100 shares (denomination flexibility)
- Metadata dictates trade terms: counterparties, times, constraints
- Same NFT could trade against bananas (or anything with metadata)
- No need for rigid, separate trading venues
- **Question for DTCC:** Does DTCC become a protocol that defines metadata standards?

Brian's response: That's possible but not the near-term path; starting with securities, tokenizing $100T, then pursuing further development through partnership.

### AI and Decentralized Protocols
Michael raises "elephant in the room": AI agents operating in programmable finance. Joe addresses:

**Current state:**
- Centralized organizations building foundational models and user interfaces
- Big tech has been valuable but also polarizing (especially social media)

**Protocols being built (ConsenSys, Ethereum Foundation):**
- Agents can pay one another
- Agents register capabilities and reputation
- Humans and companies provide feedback on agent performance

**Larger macro context:**
- 80-year post-WWII debt super cycle ending
- Next cycle will be dominated by: AI + decentralized protocols
- AI enables real agency (financial, economic, social, political)

**Personal vision (Joe):**
- Everyone will have a "digital twin" AI agent (tutor, mentor)
- Agent has one main controller (you)
- Can represent you in Web2 and Web3
- Connects you to biological and machine intelligences
- Will filter information (reducing polarity and fake news)
- Future: humans will work in collectives with AI representatives

**Singularity:** Joe believes we're moving rapidly toward it, but it will be a "hybridization of machine and biological intelligence."

### Trust and Human Agency in the AI World
Michael pushes on institutional responsibility. The tension:
- DTCC is announcing a "Summit on Human Agency" (Feb 23, in partnership with LF Decentralized Trust)
- Key principle: Human authority must be the base layer in AI systems
- All other trust infrastructures must start with that

**Brian's closing on DTCC's role:**
- "If trust in what we do fails, there's no sense in having DTCC as an organization"
- DTCC is critical to providing ecosystem trust
- Lowering barriers to engagement (tokenization) is only viable because firms trust DTCC's motives
- **Unique positioning:** DTCC's ownership structure and governance are aligned with industry benefit, not commercial interests
  - Not driven by commercial profits
  - Driven by what's good for the industry
  - This is DTCC's value proposition in the AI era

## Key Decisions
- **Tokenization approach:** DTCC will enable $100T in asset tokenization, maintaining KYC/AML standards and regulatory compliance
- **Multi-chain strategy:** No lock-in to single Layer 1 network
- **Partnership model:** DTCC will develop through inclusive partnerships, not unilateral expansion
- **Trust as foundation:** Human agency and institutional trust must underpin AI innovation
