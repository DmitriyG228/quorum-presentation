# Tether

- **Description:** Cryptocurrency stablecoin issuer; major provider of USD-pegged stablecoin (USDT)
- **Industry:** Cryptocurrency, Stablecoins
- **Involvement:** Primary offshore stablecoin issuer; focus of Congressional regulatory debates

## Key Characteristics
- Primarily offshore-based (not US-regulated)
- Largest stablecoin by market capitalization historically
- Reserve backing questioned and controversial
- Subject of Federal Reserve and regulatory concerns

## Role in Stablecoin Policy
- Represents "wildcat money" concerns in Congressional policy debates
- Tether's operational opacity and reserve questions drove Congressional appetite for US-based, regulated stablecoin alternatives
- Key target of regulatory frameworks like HR 4766 and Senate bills

## Policy Impact
- Congressional bills designed to enable US-based stablecoin issuers partly motivated by desire to displace offshore alternatives like Tether
- [[Franklin Noll]] notes that US-based stable coins could "push back against Tether and other offshore stable coins"
- Seigniorage concerns: Though Tether's volume is small relative to overall financial system, some worry about lost Fed revenue
