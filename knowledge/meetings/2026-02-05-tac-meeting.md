### Mentorship Program 2026

**Program Announcement**
[[Men]] (Program Manager at Linux Foundation) presented the 10th year of LF Decentralized Trust mentorship program. Key information:

- **Program dates:** Early February through mid-March (application period); June through September (mentorship period)
- **Mentee onboarding:** First two weeks of June
- **Mentee-mentor pairing:** Mentees work with mentors from June through completion (~4 months)

**Program Benefits:**
- Attracts talent from academia and industry into open source communities
- Previous mentees become active contributors, maintainers, and join member company teams
- Creates virtuous cycle where mentees become mentors
- Provides clear pathways for getting involved in open source projects
- Offers recognition opportunities and conference travel funding

**Financial Support:**
- Mid-term and final stipends for mentees
- Limited travel fund budget for exceptional mentors and mentees with financial need to attend in-person events

**Expectations for Mentors:**
- Provide ongoing guidance and feedback throughout mentorship period
- Model open source community practices
- Introduce mentees to broader project community
- Clear project scope and well-articulated learning objectives required

### Virtual Maintainer Focus Event Discussion

**Challenge:** Not all maintainers can attend in-person events due to travel approval constraints, timing conflicts, and global distribution.

**Proposed Approach:** Create discrete virtual events complementing in-person gatherings:
- Virtual "office hours" or informal discussion spaces (coffee/whiteboard conversations)
- Follow-up events on specific topics identified at maintainer days
- Live-streaming option from in-person events for remote participation
- Avoid duplicating in-person presentations; focus on interactive/collaborative content

**Valued Elements (per participant feedback):**
- Ad hoc informal chats more valuable than formal presentations
- Immersive experience of in-person events (focused learning without daily distractions)
- Open coffee/drop-in discussion format (no required attendance)
- Cross-project collaboration discussions

**Next Steps:** [[David]] to compile maintainer days content and share summary at next TAC meeting for broader discussion.
