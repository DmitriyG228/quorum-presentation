# Libra

- **Description:** Cryptocurrency/stablecoin project proposed by Meta (formerly Facebook) in 2019
- **Status:** Deprecated (later rebranded and largely abandoned)
- **Maintained by:** [[Meta]]

## Key Details
- **White Paper Release:** June 2019
- **Proposed Backing:** Basket of multiple currencies (eventually single-currency backing in Libra 2.0)
- **Consortium Model:** 40 founding companies including [[Visa]] and [[PayPal]]
- **Original Governance:** Switzerland-based association
- **Libra 2.0 (June 2020):** Rebranded to address Congressional concerns about managed coins and multi-currency baskets; proposed multiple single-currency stablecoins instead
- **Later Name:** Rebranded to [[Diem]] (but momentum lost)
- **End Date:** Abandoned by end of 2020

## Congressional Opposition
- Led by [[Maxine Waters]] (House) and [[Sherrod Brown]] (Senate) — both Democrats
- Key concern: Establishment of "shadow bank" with Swiss governance that could rival US dollar and cause financial instability (echoing 2008 financial crisis concerns)
- [[Sylvia Garcia]] proposed classifying as securities via HR 5197
- Quote from Maxine Waters: "This will be a new Swiss-based financial system that is too big to fail"

## Policy Impact
- Libra/Diem proposal served as catalyst for reframing stable coins in US policy discourse
- Its failure and abandonment enabled narrative shift from "ban stablecoins" to "regulate stablecoins"
- Enabled regulatory opening that Brian Brooks at OCC would later capitalize on
